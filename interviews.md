# User Interviews

### Interview 1

- **Name/Initials:** Aditi Raut (my sister)
- **Role:** Software Engineer II
- **Company stage:** JPMorgan Chase (large enterprise, but her team has their own AI tool budget)
- **3+ direct quotes:**
  1. "I pay for Cursor Pro myself, $20 a month. The team officially uses Copilot Enterprise, but it's slower."
  2. "Wait... I never thought about whether Copilot Enterprise for a 12-person team is the right plan. Nobody asked us."
  3. "I didn't even know Windsurf existed. If it's free, why am I paying for Cursor?"
  4. "Honestly, I'd switch if the company let me, but they picked Enterprise for security. Maybe the audit could suggest a cheaper enterprise plan."
- **Most surprising thing they said:** She double‑pays for two coding tools—one corporate, one personal—and treats it as normal. She'd never run the numbers.
- **What it changed about my design:** The audit needed to handle cases where one developer uses more than one tool for the same use case. Added a mental note for "consolidation" as a future rule.

### Interview 2

- **Name/Initials:** Vaibhav Londhe (my brother)
- **Role:** Automation Engineer
- **Company stage:** Mid‑size automation company (50 employees), uses AI for research and script generation
- **3+ direct quotes:**
  1. "We use ChatGPT Plus for research and Claude Pro for writing automation scripts. I think we spend $40 per person total."
  2. "Are you serious? I could use the API directly and save money? I always thought APIs were more expensive."
  3. "What do you mean 'buy credits'? That sounds sketchy. Is that actually legit?"
  4. "Most of my team doesn't even use the advanced features. We just need basic chat and code generation."
- **Most surprising thing they said:** He didn't trust the concept of discounted credits. It sounded like a scam to him initially.
- **What it changed about my design:** I realized the Credex credit recommendation needs a credibility layer—maybe a brief explainer of how discounted credits actually work (overforecasting, bulk deals) so users trust it.

### Interview 3

- **Name/Initials:** Aditya Dongare (my senior from college)
- **Role:** CTO & Co‑founder
- **Company stage:** Early‑stage startup (6 employees), just raised a small seed round
- **3+ direct quotes:**
  1. "Bro, we spend $600 a month on AI stuff and I've never actually added it up before. This audit made me physically cringe."
  2. "Wait, the Gemini API has a free tier? I'm paying $20 for Gemini Pro and the API is free up to a point? Oh no."
  3. "I'm sending this to my co‑founder right now. Can you make it a PDF? He doesn't click links."
  4. "We're definitely switching at least two things. What's the catch? How do you make money off this?"
- **Most surprising thing they said:** He wanted a PDF export immediately—not for himself, but because his co‑founder "doesn't click links." Sharing format matters.
- **What it changed about my design:** PDF export jumped from a "nice‑to‑have bonus" to a clear feature request. I noted it in my reflection. Also, his suspicion about monetization confirmed that the lead capture has to feel transparent and low‑pressure.
