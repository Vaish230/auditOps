# Go‑to‑Market Strategy

## Exact target user

**CTO or Engineering Manager** at a seed‑stage or Series‑A startup (5‑50 employees) that pays for at least three AI tools. They are technical enough to care about product quality, but cost‑conscious because runway matters. They frequently sign off on SaaS subscriptions and are tired of creeping AI tool bills with no benchmark.

## What they Google or scroll through before wanting this tool

- "startup AI tool stack cost"
- "Cursor vs GitHub Copilot price"
- "how much do startups spend on ChatGPT"
- Scrolling through **Hacker News** threads about rising SaaS costs
- Reading **Lenny’s Newsletter** or **The Pragmatic Engineer** about engineering budget management

## Where they hang out online (specific channels)

- **Reddit:** r/startups, r/SaaS, r/engineering
- **Slack groups:** Launch MBA, Indie Worldwide, CTO Craft
- **Discord:** The Developer Health OS, Reactiflux
- **X lists:** @lennysan’s “SaaS founders”, @shreyas’s “startup ops”
- **LinkedIn:** Engineering leadership groups, startup CTO circles

## How I’d get the first 100 users in 30 days with $0 budget

1. **Cold outreach (50 users):** Personally DM 10‑15 startup CTOs per day on X and LinkedIn with a specific audit snippet for their stack (I’d look at their company’s tools page first). Each DM would include a link to a pre‑filled audit result (e.g., “I saw you use Cursor + Claude; you could save $40/month.”)
2. **Community posting (30 users):** Write a short, useful post in r/startups and Indie Worldwide Slack titled “I built a free AI spend audit tool (took 2 minutes)” and include a screenshot. Reply to every comment asking about specific tools.
3. **Wave 2: referrals (20 users):** Add a “Share this audit” button with pre‑written tweet copy. Users who share get a “free quarterly re‑audit” when more tools are added.

## The unfair distribution channel

Credex already has relationships with founders buying discounted AI credits. They could embed a “Run a free audit of your whole stack” link in every invoice or onboarding email, instantly putting the tool in front of thousands of warm leads — people already proven to care about AI spend. No cold startup could replicate this without Credex’s existing customer base.

## What week‑1 traction looks like if this works

- **Week 1:** 100 audits completed, 60 unique emails captured, 10 consultation bookings, 1‑2 credit purchases ($500‑$2000 ARR each)
- **Audit‑to‑lead conversion:** >60% (since lead capture appears after value is shown)
- **Twitter/Reddit:** 1 post with >50 upvotes/comments, bringing organic traffic
